Welcome!

Welcome to Methods in R, a topic reference for biomedical research and peer review.

About this book

The R / biostatistics shelf is crowded with linear textbooks. Methods in R is shaped differently: it expects to serve two readers in the same week — the author who has data in front of them and needs to pick a method, run it correctly in R, and write the Methods paragraph; and the peer reviewer who has a manuscript on their desk and needs to know what a competent application of the method looks like, which diagnostics should have been reported, and what red flags to flag.

Every method page therefore ends with a For Reviewers section: the misapplications, the missing diagnostics, the red flags in tables and figures, and the things to verify against the authors’ references and arithmetic. The book opens at Chapter 1, a decision tree from research question to method.

The body covers sixteen topic areas — from foundations and inference through regression, survival, Bayesian methods, machine learning, bioinformatics, and study design. Hands-on labs consolidate the methods of each area into a single runnable file. A final part, Reporting Templates, gives copy-paste Methods and Results paragraphs tagged by STROBE / CONSORT / TRIPOD-AI / PRISMA / ARRIVE.

After reading, you should be able to (a) pick a defensible method for a given research question, (b) run it in R with diagnostics you can defend at peer review, and (c) write up a Methods paragraph that meets the relevant reporting standard.

How to use this book

  • Linear vs. lookup. If you already know the method, jump to its chapter. If not, start at Chapter 1 and follow the decision tree.
  • Where the code lives. Code in each method page and lab is display-only in the rendered book; copy it into an R session with the packages cited in that section attached. Reusable helpers live in R/helpers.R in the source repository.
  • Corrections and suggestions. Open an issue at https://github.com/r-heller/methods-in-r/issues.
  • Per-chapter PDFs. Every chapter has a Download this chapter (PDF) button at the top of its page (rendered alongside the HTML on each push to main).
  • Whole-book PDF and EPUB. Use the Download menu in the navbar (top of every page) for a single-file PDF or EPUB of the whole book.
